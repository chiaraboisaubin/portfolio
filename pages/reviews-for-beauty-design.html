Context

This project was to think of methods to provide a personal feel to the shopping experience by making the reviews customizable, specifically for beauty products. This was an opportunity to make the experience use inclusive methods when asking people to describe their physical features like skin complexion and hair type.

The goals for this project are

  • To make writing and reading reviews customizable
  • Increase loyalty and new customers
  • Not offend people, isolate group(s) of potential shoppers
  • See how we can make the experience inclusive with existing scales for describing skin and hair
  • Prove that we are finding ways to demonstrate our values, not just talking about them

Content

We used the Andrew Walker and F.I.A hair systems for phrasing for hair since they identify different parts of hair. We used the Von Lushan scale for skin tone, allowing us to identify more than the tone, but the undertones. These were the most commonly used; some people already know about them.

User testing

With the user testing, we had 10 participants :

  • 3 out of 10 participants identified as Cauascains, and the rest were either multi-racial, identified as black/African American, or other. 
  • When it came to finding a foundation, 7 of 10 participants said they had difficulty knowing what some companies meant with the names of the shades.
  • They would find and stick with a product, using the ratings and reviews to help with their shopping experience.
  • For hair products, a range of users found it easy to find products for their hair type, or there needed to be more clarity.

Takeaways

Did we answer our questions?

Did we achieve our goals?

Some existing systems did find some method

What are the next steps?

  • Waiting on a third-party organization survey that gathered data to learn more about people’s preference for describing themselves, if the beauty industry is becoming more inclusive, and shopping.
  • Find a way to teach users how to identify their skin and hair
