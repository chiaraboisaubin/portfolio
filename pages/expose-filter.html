Context

This project initially was a build-a-basket project to increase users' activity with the filtering for the shopping experience. However, it slowly became something that the enterprise wanted to improve. I also wanted to implement a horizontal/exposing filter.

User problems

People were not using filters throughout the CVS experience. Users want a simple way to filter information and interaction. To do so, we need to see how we can make the experience feel less overwhelming, and for us to do that, we as designers have to adjust the interaction and function for the different platforms, understand the current experience, and learn how to improve the experience for e-commerce and scheduling.

Initial goal

  • Make global components and update elements
  • Determine why users were not using the filtering experience and see if it is valuable
  • Build-a-basket needed a couple of steps to achieve and organize with other teams to start working on the filtering
  • Ensuring the experience is native to each platform also improves accessibility

Desk research

Our analytics gather within less than 10% of the users. From past user testing from the UXR team and other designers, the exposed filter had value with the user for specific information. The research, however, could have gone more in-depth on what participants typically look for in the filter experience.

Looking at Beymard research and e-commerce recommendations, some information assisted with improving the experience.

Looking at companies that have an exposed filter, things that were noticed:

  • When looking at specific information, like star rating or categories
  • There was some type a content hierarchy of information
  • The maximum category and feature in the horizontal experience was 10
  • They used them to do quick filters and use pills for the info
  • We looked at the filtering experience for shopping
  • Most companies did use native expertise when it came to the flow of the experience and the user interface

Wireframes

With the information that was gathered from the user testing, the goals of all specialties, and the CVS persona that already existed, me and other designers worked together to see how to make the information flow and not repeat information. The wireframe was also the space where we ensured that people who were a part of the project and other teams were aligned on how data would be presented.

Takeaways
