Competitive analysis

We decided to conduct user testing with the recent experience to see if the information made sense to users and if participants could navigate to the dashboard and certain features like updating their membership. I did competitive research on what other companies did for their membership or dashboard pages.

The companies looked into were:
  • Walmart
  • Amazon
  • Target
  • Adobe

Things that was noticed

  • Maxiumim of two pages with information that connects with the membership
  • There was some type a content hierarchy of information
  • Ability to show other memeberships and subscription

UX analysis

Baymard provided feedback on the CVS shopping experience that would help connect the UX e-commerce experience. Some of their callouts were to make sure that we have the ability for users to go back to the main page of content if they were navigated to another page and have information have some flow. Looking at the current membership of Carepass, we noticed that getting to the dashboard page was not as accessible. The content was misleading or not as clear and repetitive content on the same page.

User testing

We interviewed five participants who were current Carepass members and had done user testing in the past. We also looked for users who had a type of disability that they needed technical assistance. Our goals:

  • See their expectations regarding the dashboard
  • Gather their thoughts on the current information about Carepass on the page.
  • If the participants could navigate to the dashboard page

With the data that was collected from the testing, we learned the following:

  1. 3 out of 5 participants suggested reorganizing the content and the information that needed clarification.
  2. 2 participants thought that the cancelation call to action should be more visible.
  3. 2 participants users said there was significant white space on the page.
  4. All participants identify seeing membership information as the top 3 important to them.
  5. All participants responded that they were satisfied with the current. Dashboard content.
Even though we conducted user testing and user interviews, we used the CVS persona Micheal to sync up with CVS to ensure we were going with other users' goals.

Wireframes

With the information that was gathered from the user testing, the goals of all specialties, and the CVS persona that already existed, me and other designers worked together to see how to make the information flow and not repeat information. The wireframe was also the space where we ensured that people who were a part of the project and other teams were aligned on how data would be presented.

High-fidelity prototypes

At this time, Carepass was also introducing a new feature for gaining rewards with the amount of steps a users take. Leadership wanted to make the feature apart of the dashboard. We that additional information we needed the following experiences:

  • New user
  • Existing user with or without the reward
  • Able to add new features
  • Showing avaible promoitons that connects with the memebership - for example, the monthly free $10

Examples

New user, who has not shopped, no Healthy Rewards, and $10 available

Manage member page

Once we agreed on how we wanted the information, I started working on the user interface. I made sure that we were designing mobile-first, responsive design, implementing the CVS UI kit and components, and staying as close as possible to the Carepass brand. Mobile was less challenging than desktop, and there was some disagreement of the style for simplicity but ensuring that there was the ability to add more programs and features to the page. We also had to deal with accessibility calls out.

Takeaways

Did we answer our questions?

I believe that we were able resturct the information so it is easy to find the common information users was looking for

Did we achieve our goals?

There were a lot of challenges with this project like Figma was new to the team, a lot of different departments and people feedback that needed to stay with the project's goals, and the tight deadline. I believe we did achieve the goals of simplicity, making sure that we do best accessible and design practices and the ability to add information in the future.
